Most crypto prop firms publish no verifiable data — pass rates are claimed, payouts are claimed, challenge purchases are claimed. Hyro publishes on-chain everything an LP needs to evaluate a vault.

## Verifiable from Phase 1 onward

<Check>**Every challenge purchase** — fee → fee vault TX hash</Check>
<Check>**Every funded-demo payout** — payout vault → trader wallet TX hash</Check>
<Check>**Every vault performance-fee crystallization** — with HWM reference</Check>
<Check>**Every LP deposit / withdrawal** — with NAV at execution</Check>
<Check>**Every vault opening / closing event**</Check>
<Check>**Aggregate metrics** — total payouts, active vaults, total AUM, fee vault balance, payout vault balance</Check>

All of the above are linked to Solana explorer for independent verification.

## What is NOT claimed as on-chain

<Note>
HyroTrader has paid out $5M+ to traders historically. Those legacy payouts were settled **before** on-chain settlement infrastructure was live. Hyro does **not** retroactively claim on-chain verifiability for them. All Hyro Protocol payouts from Phase 1 onward are on-chain natively, each with a TXID.
</Note>

This honesty is deliberate. Claiming verifiability you can't back up is exactly the kind of thing the protocol exists to replace.

## A vault's economics are public

Because policy and fee logic are pluggable on-chain pubkeys, a vault's entire configuration is auditable before you deposit:

- `policy_program` — which authorization rules apply
- `fee_calc_program` — which fee strategy and rates apply
- The dispatcher's `total_collected` / `total_claimed` ledger — every fee ever charged
