One of the core problems with traditional prop firms is rule changes mid-evaluation. Hyro solves this by treating the rulebook as a versioned, public artifact with a verifiable change history.

## How it works

<Steps>
  <Step title="Every version is published">
    Each rule version lives at `hyro.network/rulebook` with a version number (e.g. `v1.0`), an effective date, and a git-style commit hash.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Rules apply prospectively only">
    Challenges purchased under `v1.0` are evaluated against `v1.0` rules even after `v1.1` is published. The rule version is recorded **on-chain** at challenge purchase.
  </Step>
  <Step title="The changelog is public">
    Every change since `v1.0` is documented with date, scope, and rationale. LPs, traders, and investors can audit the entire rule-change history.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Material changes require notice">
    Meaningful updates are announced via Discord, X, and email with minimum lead time before taking effect.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## The guarantee

<Check>
Because the rule version is pinned on-chain at purchase, "rules cannot change mid-evaluation" is a **structural guarantee** enforced by the protocol — not a promise that can be quietly broken.
</Check>
